An Example of RCT

  • Consider a randomized controlled trial with a continuous endpoint
  • The goal is to show that the treatment is superior than the control

Group sequential test

  • A group sequential test monitors a statistic summarizing the difference in primary responses between the treatment and control groups at a series of times during the trial
  • At interim analyses, if the value of this statistic exceeds some specified boundary, the trial is stopped and the null hypothesis is rejected
  • Interim analyses the trial to be stopped early with a valid conclusion
    • Participants are not exposed to ineffective or unsafe treatments (ethical)
    • Assumptions made at the design stage are checked (administrative)
    • Sample size, time and cost are not wasted (economic)

Information fraction

  • Defined as the fraction of total information expected at the scheduled end of the trial
  • Roughly, \(\mbox{IF} = {n_k \over N}\)

Boundaries

  • Pocock
    • Constant for all analyses: \(c(K)\)
    • Narrower initially, easier to stop early
  • O'Brien-Fleming
    • Narrower boundaries at later analysis (i.e., conservative initially): \(c(K) / \sqrt{k}\)
    • Similar boundary at the final analysis to the fixed sample test

Alpha?

  • Nominal significance level at the \(k\)th interim analysis
    • \(\alpha_k = \Pr(|Z_k| \geq c_k)\)
    • Probability of rejecting the null hypothesis at the \(k\)th interim analysis
  • The error spent at stage \(k\)
    • \(\pi_k = \Pr(|Z_1| < c_1, \ldots, |Z_k| \geq c_k)\)
    • Probability of not rejecting \(H_0\) before the \(k\)th interim analysis and rejecting the null hypothesis at the \(k\)th interim analysis
  • Cumulative alpha
    • \(\alpha = \pi_1 + \ldots + \pi_K\)
    • \(\alpha \neq \pi_1 + \ldots + \pi_K\)

Alpha spending function

  • Any function can be an alpha spending function if the function satisfies
    • \(\alpha(0)=0\) (value equals \(0\) at the beginning of the study)
    • \(\alpha(1)=\alpha\) (value equals \(alpha\) at the final analysis)
    • Non-decreasing
  • A more flexible group sequential procedure that does not require the total number nor the exact time of the interim analyses to be specified in advance
