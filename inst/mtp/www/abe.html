Notation

Let \(T=0\) and \(1\) denote the antibody and placebo arm respectively. Let \(Y=1\) denote a case and \(Y=0\) otherwise.

Relative Risk and Antibody Efficacy

The risk on the antibody arm is \(P(Y=1|T=1)\); the risk on the control arm is \(P(Y=1|T=0)\). The relative risk is \[ \mbox{RR} = {P(Y=1|T=1) \over P(Y=1|T=0)}.\]

Define the antibody efficacy as \[\mbox{AbE} = 1 - RR.\]

Binomial Split

Given data, let \(N\) denote the number of cases on both arms, and \(V\) the number of cases on the antibody arm (\(T=1\)). Then, \(V|N\) can be assumed to follow a Binomial distribution \[V \sim \mbox{Binomial}(N, p).\]

Based on the definition

\begin{align*} p =& P(Y = 1, T = 1|Y=1) \\ =& {P(Y=1, T=1) \over P(Y=1)} \\ =& {P(Y=1| T=1) P(T=1) \over P(Y=1|T=1) P(T=1) + P(Y=1|T=0) P(T=0)} \\ =& {RR \times P(T=1) \over RR \times P(T=1) + P(T=0)} \\ =& {(1 - AbE) \times P(T=1) \over (1 - AbE) \times P(T=1) + P(T=0)} \\ \end{align*}

Specifically, when the randomization ratio is 1:1, we have \(P(T=1) = P(T=0)\) and \[p = {1 - AbE \over 2 - AbE}.\]
